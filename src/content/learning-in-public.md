---
title: "On learning in public without performing knowledge"
date: 2026-03-28
category: Career Reflections
excerpt: "Writing to understand, not to look like I already understand. The difference shows up in every sentence."
---

There is a version of “learning in public” that is really performative — publishing polished opinions to signal expertise. I wanted the opposite: notes that admit what confused me, written while I was still figuring it out.

The test I use: would I still write this sentence if nobody read it? If it's there to impress, it usually goes. What's left is the actual reasoning — the wrong turn I took, the thing that finally clicked.

Writing this way is slower and more exposing, but it's the only kind of writing that has ever taught me anything.
